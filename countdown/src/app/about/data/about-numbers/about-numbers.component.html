Objective

The objective of the numbers round is to get as close as you can to a randomly-generated three-digit number using six randomly selected numbers and the most basic four mathematical operators (addition, subtraction, division, and multiplication).

How to Play

To begin, select how many large numbers you would like. The large numbers are 25, 50, 75, and 100. Each large number exists only once in the large number deck, so if you select one large number, it is liable to be any of those four, but if you select four large numbers, you are certain to get one of each. The rest of the six numbers will be randomly drawn from the small number deck, consisting of the numbers one through ten.

After the numbers are generated, a timer will count down from 30 seconds, and you will be prompted to enter your result. In this box, enter the number you can achieve with the given numbers and four basic operators that is closest to the target number. You may only submit one entry, so be careful!

Ash spots that she can make exactly 532, and enters 532 into the “enter your result” box. She enters her equation as (75*7)+8+2-3, and scores 10 points for hitting the target number exactly!
Note that she did not have to use all six numbers, and could repeat operators used—just not numbers themselves.

You will receive ten points for hitting the target exactly, seven points for being within 5, five points for being within 10, and zero points for anything farther than 10 away from the target number (or an incorrect equation).

Please note that your equation must evaluate to an integer, your solution may only involve each number as many times as it is shown on the screen, and that your solutions do not need to use all six numbers given.

Once the timer expires and you have submitted your result, you will be taken to a screen where you can input your equation using numbers, basic operators, and parentheses. Make sure your equation equals exactly the number you entered in the “enter your result” box, or you will score zero points!
