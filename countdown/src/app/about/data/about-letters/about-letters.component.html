Objective

The objective of the letters round is to make the longest word you can using only the nine letters randomly revealed to you.

How to Play

To begin, select whether you would like your next letter to be a vowel or a consonant until you have selected a total of nine letters. Each set of nine letters selected must have a minimum of three vowels and four consonants. Once the ninth letter has been revealed, a 30-second timer begins.

During this time, you may enter as many valid letter combinations into the “enter a word” box as you wish, bearing in mind that you will only be submitting one word at the end of the timer. A valid letter combination is any combination that uses only the letters on the board only as many times as they appear.

In this example, you could make the word “LAMA,” as there are two As on the board, but not “LLAMA,” as there is only one L. Submitting the word "LAMA" would grant you a score of 4 points.

Please note that at this stage of the game, you will not receive any verification as to whether or not your chosen combination of letters is in the dictionary.

At the end of the 30-second timer, you will be taken to a screen that prompts you to select the word you would like to submit. You may submit only one word, and scoring is based on word length, so select the longest word you have made that you are confident is a word — or take a gamble if you’re feeling lucky! Common word game rules apply: no proper nouns or abbreviations will be accepted.

Scoring is as follows: zero points for an invalid word or no submission, one point per letter of the submitted valid word, and a whopping 18 points if you manage to make a word with all nine letters!
